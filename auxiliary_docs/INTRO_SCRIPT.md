## CORRELATION EXPLANATION (Choose One)

### Option A: Quick Technical Connection

> "Here's how it works: the game runs on Arduino. Every room has defined LED values - the Red Cavern sets red to 200, the Green Chamber sets green to 255. When you move, the LEDs change. When you pick up a memory crystal, the corresponding LED pulses. When the enemy appears, they all go dark. The physical world responds to the digital story in real-time."

### Option B: Emotional Connection

> "The LEDs aren't a gimmick - they're the character. Helena's consciousness is split across three channels. When you're in the Red Cavern, you're in her anger and fear - watch the red LED spike. In the Blue Maze, you're in her fragmented logic - the blue LED blinks faster as you get closer to the solution. In the Green Chamber, you're in her identity, her sense of self - the green LED breathes slowly, peacefully. You're not just playing a game - you're navigating a broken mind."

### Option C: Development Story

> "I started with a detailed system design - every room, every object, every LED response mapped out. Then I gave it to Kiro and said 'create a spec for this.' Kiro broke it into formal requirements, 42 correctness properties, and 25 implementation tasks. Then it built the entire game end-to-end. The LEDs, the parser, the enemy AI, the memory optimization - all from those specs. That's the power of spec-driven development."

## VISUAL SUGGESTIONS

### For Your Intro Recording:

**Setup:**
- You on camera (main focus)
- Arduino visible on desk or in hand
- Good lighting on both you and Arduino
- LEDs should be visible (can be off or showing a pattern)

**Key Moments to Show:**
1. **Point to Arduino** when you mention it
2. **Point to each LED** when you say "red, blue, green"
3. **Hold up Arduino** for dramatic effect
4. **Gesture to screen** when transitioning to gameplay

**Lighting:**
- Bright on you (so you're clearly visible)
- Slightly dimmer on Arduino (so LEDs show up)
- No backlighting (window behind you)

## RECOMMENDED APPROACH

**Use Version 3 (Story-First) with Option B (Emotional Connection)**

**Why:**
- Most gripping opening
- Clearly explains the LED correlation
- Shows the innovation (physical feedback for digital story)
- Emphasizes originality (not a port)
- Highlights Kiro usage
- Creates emotional investment

**Timing:**
- 0:00-0:30: Your intro (Version 3)
- 0:30-2:45: Gameplay with voiceover

## PRACTICE TIPS

1. **Memorize the key beats**, not word-for-word
2. **Pause for emphasis** - especially after "She's wrong" or "They're her"
3. **Point at things** - Arduino, LEDs, screen
4. **Vary your tone** - start mysterious, build to excited
5. **End with energy** - "Let me show you" should sound inviting
